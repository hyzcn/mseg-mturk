Our goal is to classify the highlighted-in-pink mask (blob of pixels).

You will see 2 versions of the same image, arranged in a row. From left to right, they will be the original image, then the blob of pixels highlighted in pink in the image. Sometimes the pink pixels will be hard to find. You will sometimes need to zoom in to see clearly.

We will show a few examples of each category below:

stool: a simple seat without a back or arms; or a high seat at a bar without arms

seat: a space reserved for sitting (as in a theater, auditorium, on a train or airplane, or in a car). (that is not a bench, chair, stool, sofa, or armchair)

swivel-chair: a chair that swivels on its base. Can be turned around a central point to face in a different direction without moving the legs

armchair: chair with a support on each side for arms (that is not a swivel chair, seat, or sofa)

chair-other: Any chair that does NOT fall into the following categories: armchair, stool, seat, sofa. A chair is defined as a seat for one person, with a support for the back.
